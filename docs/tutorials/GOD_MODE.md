### 2.3 Targeted Entity Intervention âš¡

Directly manipulate individual organisms to guide evolution or eliminate threats.

| Key | Action | Description |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| `M` | **Mutate** | Apply random genetic modifications to selected entity |
| `K` | **Smite** | Remove selected entity from simulation |

#### Mutation Effects (`M`)
When you mutate an entity, the following traits may change:
- **Physical**: Sensing range, max speed, max energy capacity
- **Metabolic**: Metabolic niche (herbivore/carnivore balance)
- **Neural**: Brain topology (new neurons/connections), weight adjustments
- **Life History**: Reproductive investment, maturity rate

#### Reincarnation Effects (`P`)
Reincarnation completely randomizes the entity's HexDNA while preserving its identity. This is useful for:
- Introducing radically new traits into a stagnant lineage
- Creating "challenge runs" where ancient entities face modern conditions
- Testing genetic compatibility between distant lineages

## 4. Walkthrough: Breeding a Master Race ğŸ§¬

Follow these steps to accelerate the evolution of a superior lineage:

1. **Isolate**: Use the **Wall brush (`5`)** to trap a promising population in a small, controlled area.
2. **Pressure**: Apply environmental stress. Use the **Barren brush (`6`)** to limit food, or create a **War Zone (`@`)** to introduce predation pressure. Only the strongest should survive.
3. **Mutate**: Press **`X`** to trigger a **Genetic Surge**, or select promising individuals and press **`M`** for targeted mutation. Watch for favorable mutationsâ€”higher speed, better sensing, or increased aggression.
4. **Select**: When a "Master" emerges, left-click to select it. Use **`C`** to export its HexDNA or **`W`** to save the world state.
5. **Refine**: Use **`P`** (Reincarnate) on underperformers to inject fresh genetic material, or **`K`** (Smite) to remove weak specimens.
